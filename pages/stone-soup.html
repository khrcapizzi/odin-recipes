Stone Soup

About:

Stone soup is my version of zuppa Toscana, a classic Italian dish. I learned this recipe from my mother and in my early twenties I came to depend on it as a cheap, comforting dish I could make with as few or as many ingredients as I had available. I came to call it stone soup because once the base of the soup is prepared, almost anything can be added to create a satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • kale
  • red potatoes
  • red onion
  • garlic
  • rigatino/bacon
  • celery
  • carrot
  • cannellini beans
  • tomato pulp
  • salt
  • powdered or crushed red chile pepper
  • black pepper

As you can see, there are many ingredients listed here. Fear not, because only the basics are absolutely necessary!

Preparation

  1. Sauté the rigatino in a large cast iron pot using a small amount of your favorite high smoke point cooking oil
  2. Remove rigatino and set aside. Remove oil and set aside to measure out 2tbsp to return to the pot.
  3. In the same pot, sauté your chopped onion, carrots, and celery together. Sprinkle with salt and pepper while cooking.
  4. Add minced garlic.
  5. Add 1 quart chicken stock and bring to simmer
  6. Once simmering, add potatoes, beans, rigatino, and tomato pulp.
  7. Simmer for 50 minutes then add kale.
  8. Serve immediately or store in the fridge or freezer to enjoy throughout your week!
